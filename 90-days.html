Immigration reporting

90-Day Report

Report your current residential address when you remain in Thailand for more than 90 consecutive days. This is separate from extending your permission to stay.

A 90-day report is not a visa extension

Submitting TM.47 reports your address. It does not extend your permission to stay, replace TM.30 accommodation notification, or give permission to work.

Understand the three different requirements

Requirement What it does Who normally completes it
90-Day Report (TM.47) Reports your current residential address after 90 consecutive days in Thailand. The foreign national or an authorized representative, subject to the submission method.
Extension of stay Requests a new date until which you may legally remain in Thailand. The student applies with BU supporting documents and Immigration approval.
Accommodation notification (TM.30) Notifies Immigration where a foreign national is accommodated. The householder, property owner, possessor, or accommodation provider when required.

Check your reporting window

The standard in-person reporting window is from 15 days before the due date through 7 days after the due date.

Date example

If the due date shown on your latest receipt is 30 September, the standard window is 15 September through 7 October. Do not interpret “90 days” as exactly three calendar months.

Online reports must be submitted before the due date

The official online system accepts an advance request within 15 days before the next due date. Do not wait until the seven-day late period to attempt an online submission.

Choose a reporting method

Use the official TM.47 online system

  1. Register or sign in using the official website.
  2. Submit a new TM.47 application within 15 days before the due date.
  3. Monitor email and the application dashboard.
  4. Download and save the approved receipt showing the next due date.

The official guide states that review may take approximately three days. When an application is not approved, contact the responsible Immigration office in person as soon as possible.

Open online 90-day reporting

Report at the responsible Immigration office

Use the office responsible for the address where you actually reside. A student extending at Pathum Thani Immigration may still need to complete the 90-day report in another province when their residence is outside Pathum Thani.

Bring the original passport, completed TM.47, latest 90-day receipt if available, and any current address evidence requested by that office.

An authorized person may submit in permitted cases

The representative should carry the required documents, the original passport where required, and authorization evidence requested by Immigration. Confirm local office practice before sending another person.

Documents commonly needed for an in-person report

  • Original passport Include the passport containing your latest entry, extension, and re-entry stamps.
  • Completed TM.47 form Use the current Immigration form and complete the residential address accurately.
  • Previous 90-day receipt Bring the latest receipt when you have reported before.
  • Current accommodation evidence Immigration may check TM.30 or other address evidence, especially when the address or passport has changed.
  • Additional local-office documents Requirements may differ by office and individual immigration record.

After leaving and re-entering Thailand

The 90-day count begins again

After re-entry, count from the latest arrival date rather than the old 90-day due date. This reset does not extend your permission to stay.

The official online guide states that a person with a recorded prior 90-day history may be able to report online after returning with a valid re-entry permit. If the system cannot match the record, complete the report in person.

When online reporting may fail

  • The information entered does not exactly match Immigration records.
  • The application is outside the online submission window.
  • A previous request using the same information remains pending.
  • You have a new passport or changed personal information.
  • Your latest entry or previous reporting history is not available to the online system.

If the report is late

Report in person

A late 90-day report normally requires an in-person report and may result in a 2,000-baht fine. A late report is different from overstay: separately confirm that your permission-to-stay date is still valid.
