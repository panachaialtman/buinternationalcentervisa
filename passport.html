Renew before requesting a long extension

An extension cannot normally be granted beyond the passport expiry date. Renewing first may prevent your student extension or ED Plus period from being shortened.

Who does what?

Responsible organization Main responsibility
Your embassy or passport authority Issues, renews, replaces, or certifies the passport.
Police Issues a report when a passport is lost or stolen.
Bangkok University Updates the student record and prepares eligible supporting documents when needed.
Thai Immigration Transfers relevant entry, extension, and re-entry information to the new passport and updates the immigration record.

Choose your situation

You still possess the old passport

Keep the old passport after receiving the new one. Contact the International Center to update your student record, then follow Immigration instructions for transferring the relevant stamps.

The passport is missing

  1. Report the loss or theft to the police.
  2. Contact your embassy for a replacement or emergency passport.
  3. Notify the International Center.
  4. Contact Immigration to reconstruct or transfer the immigration record.

Do not travel internationally until the replacement passport and Thai immigration status have been checked.

The document is unusable or personal details changed

Contact your embassy first. When a name, nationality, passport number, or other core detail changes, additional civil documents or certified evidence may be required by BU and Immigration.

Normal renewal process

  1. Renew through your embassy or passport authority

    Follow your country’s requirements and retain the old passport unless the issuing authority keeps it.

  2. Check the new passport carefully

    Verify the English name, date of birth, nationality, passport number, issue date, and expiry date.

  3. Update the BU student record

    Provide a clear copy of the new biodata page and any supporting evidence requested by the International Center or Registrar.

  4. Prepare the Immigration stamp-transfer package

    Collect both passports, embassy certification where issued, copies of relevant pages, and the Immigration transfer request.

  5. Visit the responsible Immigration office

    Use the office instructed for your immigration record. BU may provide additional supporting documents for student-status cases.

  6. Review every transferred stamp

    Check the latest entry, extension, re-entry permit or ED Plus exemption, and permission-to-stay date before leaving.

Documents commonly requested for stamp transfer

  • New passport and former passport Bring originals and copies of the biodata pages and all relevant Thai stamps.
  • Request to transfer stamps to the new passport Use the form or request format required by the Immigration office.
  • Embassy certificate or passport-renewal evidence Some offices request a letter confirming that the new passport replaces the former passport.
  • Latest entry and extension evidence Include copies of the latest arrival, permission-to-stay, extension, and re-entry information.
  • Police report Required when the former passport was lost or stolen.
  • BU supporting documents Student-status confirmation may be requested depending on the case and office.
The exact document set can differ

Passport-issuing practices vary by nationality, and Immigration offices may request additional evidence. Confirm the current list before visiting.

After the transfer

  • The old and new passport numbers are linked correctly.
  • The latest entry information is recorded.
  • The current permission-to-stay date is correct.
  • Any re-entry permit or ED Plus exemption is reflected correctly.
  • The university record shows the new passport number and expiry date.
  • Your next 90-day report uses the new passport details.
A new passport may require an in-person 90-day report

The Immigration Bureau’s general 90-day guidance states that a foreign national with a changed passport should report in person or authorize another person. Do not assume the online system will immediately recognize the new document.

Travel warning

Do not travel with an unresolved immigration record

Before departure, confirm which passport must be carried and whether the current stay and re-entry information are valid. Carry both passports when Immigration or your embassy instructs you to do so.
