Use the correct expiry date

Check the latest stamp that says when you are permitted to stay. This is the legal deadline for remaining in Thailand—not necessarily the validity date shown on a visa sticker or e-Visa document.

Eligibility check

This route is intended for students who remain actively enrolled and require an extension to continue studying.

  • Active enrolment: you are registered and continuing your programme.
  • Academic review: your academic record supports continued student status.
  • Payment status: required tuition or registration obligations are completed.
  • Valid passport: the requested period cannot normally extend beyond passport expiry.
  • No expired stay: your current permission to stay remains valid when the request is processed.
BU academic screening

Bachelor’s students are normally expected to register at least 9 credits per semester, while graduate students are normally expected to register at least 6 credits. Final-semester cases, withdrawals, failed or withdrawn courses, and other academic circumstances may require individual review.

Documents to prepare

  • Original passport Bring the current passport containing your latest Immigration stamps.
  • Latest tuition-fee receipt or registration receipt Save the digital receipt where you can access it again. Some registration receipts may only be printable once.
  • Recent photograph Use the size and recency requested by the International Center or Immigration for the current application.
  • Accommodation evidence Outside Pathum Thani: normally TM.30 evidence. Inside Pathum Thani: a valid rental contract and the property owner’s identification copy may be requested.
  • Additional academic or immigration documents The International Center or Immigration may request further evidence based on your individual record.
Initial request

You may begin the university request with your passport and submit remaining documents by the document-pickup stage when the International Center permits. Do not assume missing documents can always be submitted later.

Submit the BU request

Use the university visa-service system or visit the International Center. Keep the confirmation and check your university email, including the spam or junk folder.

Open NON-ED request system Visit or contact the office

Step-by-step process

  1. Check both passport dates

    Confirm the permission-to-stay deadline and passport expiry before requesting documents.

  2. Submit the university request

    Use the online system or contact the International Center with your passport and available supporting documents.

  3. Wait for confirmation

    The International Center reviews eligibility and informs you when the supporting documents are ready or when more information is needed.

  4. Collect the documents

    Bring your passport and any remaining required documents. A representative may collect documents only when permitted and must bring the original passport.

  5. Apply at Pathum Thani Immigration

    Submit the full application personally unless the International Center gives different case-specific instructions.

  6. Check the new stamp before leaving

    Review the passport immediately and ask the Immigration officer to correct any error before you leave the office.

Check the Immigration stamp

  • Name spelling
  • Passport number
  • Immigration status or category
  • New “permitted to stay until” date
  • Officer’s stamp and any handwritten conditions
Approval is not guaranteed

University supporting documents do not create a right to an extension. Immigration considers your complete application, academic status, passport validity, immigration history, and any additional evidence.

Employment reminder

Student immigration status does not by itself authorize employment. Any work must comply with the applicable Thai work-authorization rules.
