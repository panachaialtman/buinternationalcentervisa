NON-ED Visa Extension

  • For current students who are still studying
  • Used for study purposes only \[Illegal to work with this type of visa]
  • The extension period (6 months / 1 year / other) depends on academic status, payment status, and Immigration approval
  • Students must prepare documents before the visa expiry date

Required documents:

  1. Passport
  2. Receipt (Latest tuition fee receipt / registration receipt)
  3. Recent photo
  4. Accommodation document
    1. If you live outside Pathum Thani: TM.30
    2. If you live inside Pathum Thani: rental contract and house owner's ID card copy

You may bring only your passport first to submit the request and bring the remaining documents on the pickup date

How to submit request

  • Click here to Non-ED Online submission
  • Come to the International Center office

Step-by-Step Process

1

Check Your Visa Expiry Date

First, check the “permitted to stay until” date on your passport.

This date is very important because it shows the last day you are allowed to stay in Thailand legally. Students should not wait until the last few days before the visa expires.

2

Check Your Passport Expiry Date

Before requesting a visa extension, check your passport expiry date.

If your passport will expire soon (in 6 months or 1 year), your visa extension period may be limited by your passport validity.

If your passport is close to expiry, you may be advised to renew your passport first before applying for the visa extension.

3

Submit the Visa Extension Request

Students may submit the request through the online request system or visit the International Center in person.

Please prepare your passport and available required documents.

If some documents are not ready at the time of request submission, students may bring the remaining documents later on the document pickup date.

4

Wait for Email Confirmation

After submitting the request, students should wait for an email from the International Center.

The email will inform students when the documents are ready for pickup. Students should check their email regularly, including the spam or junk folder.

5

Pick Up the Documents

Students must come to pick up the prepared visa extension documents at the International Center.

Please bring your passport book when collecting the documents, along with any remaining documents that were not submitted earlier.

If you cannot come in person, another person may pick up the documents on your behalf. However, that person must bring your passport book with them.

6

Go to Immigration for Visa Extension

After receiving the documents, students must bring them to the Pathum Thani Immigration Office to submit the visa extension application.

Because Bangkok University is located in Pathum Thani.

7

Check the New Visa Stamp Carefully

After Immigration completes the visa extension, students must check the new stamp in the passport carefully before leaving Immigration.

Please check:

  • Name spelling
  • Passport number
  • Visa type
  • New permission of stay date
  • Immigration officer’s stamp
  • Any additional notes written by Immigration

    • If there are any mistakes, students should inform the Immigration officer immediately.

Important Notes

  • The university supporting documents do not guarantee visa approval, as final approval depends on Thai Immigration.
  • Students must not overstay. Otherwise, there will be an overstay fee.
  • Students should prepare documents at least 1 month before the visa expires
  • Students should keep copies of all important documents
  • Students should contact the International Center if they are unsure which documents are required

Restriction:

  • Students with no active enrollment may not be eligible
  • Students with academic problems, withdrawal, or (Bachelor) registered less than 9 credits may need additional review
  • If the passport will expire soon, the student should renew the passport first before applying, as the extension cannot exceed the passport expiry date
