Update / Renew Passport

Students must make sure that their passport is valid and updated before applying for any visa service in Thailand. If your passport is expired, nearly expired, damaged, lost, full, or recently renewed, you must update your passport information with the university and may need to transfer your Thai visa stamp from the old passport to the new passport.

Foreigners in Thailand who need a new passport should contact their national Embassy in Thailand. After receiving the new passport, the Embassy may provide a letter requesting Thai Immigration to transfer the latest visa stamps into the new passport, free of charge.

Who Should Read This Page?

This page is for students who:

  • Have renewed their passport
  • Plan to renew their passport soon
  • Have a passport that will expire soon
  • Have no blank pages left in the passport
  • Lost their passport
  • Have a damaged passport that cannot be read
  • Need to apply for a visa extension after receiving a new passport
  • Need to update passport information with the university

Important Restriction

A visa extension in Thailand cannot normally be granted beyond the passport expiry date. If your passport will expire soon, your visa extension period may be limited by the passport validity.

Students are strongly advised to check their passport expiry date early. If the passport will expire soon, please renew the passport before applying for a visa extension.

Case 1: Passport Will Expire Soon

Contact your Embassy in Thailand or your home country to ask about passport renewal.

After receiving your new passport, you should:

  1. Inform the International Center
  1. Check whether you need to transfer your visa stamp. If so, transfer the visa stamp at Immigration
  1. Use the new passport for your next visa extension request
Case 2: Already Received a New Passport

Please submit the following documents to the International Center:

  1. Old passport information page
  1. New passport information page
Case 3: Lost Passport

You should take action immediately. Report to the local police station and then contact their national Embassy in Thailand to apply for a new passport.

Case 4: Damaged Passport

If your passport is damaged, you should contact your Embassy or International Center to ask whether you need to renew it. A damaged passport may affect your visa extension, travel, or Immigration process.

After receiving a new passport, you may need to transfer your visa stamp from the old passport to the new passport.

Transfer Visa Stamp to New Passport

What Is Visa Stamp Transfer?

Visa stamp transfer means Thai Immigration copies or transfers the important visa and entry information from your old passport to your new passport.

This may include:

  • Latest entry stamp (date, port arrival)
  • Current visa information
  • Latest extension of stay stamp
  • Permission of stay information

Where Should Students Transfer the Visa Stamp?

Contact the Immigration Office or International Center to get the Transfer Visa Stamp forms, then bring the forms to the Pathum Thani Immigration Office.

Documents Required for Visa Stamp Transfer

Students should prepare:

  1. Old passport
  1. New passport
  1. Copy of old passport page
    1. Information page
    1. Latest arrival stamp
    1. Current visa stamp
  1. Copy of new passport information page
  1. Transfer stamp form / request form
  1. TM.30 or accommodation document, if requested
  1. 90-day report slip, if any
  1. Student ID, student certificate, or university supporting document, if requested by Immigration

Important Notes

  • Do not throw away your old passport.
  • Keep both old and new passports together until Immigration completes the transfer.
  • Do not travel abroad before checking whether your visa stamp has been transferred.
  • If your visa is still valid in the old passport, carry both passports when needed.
  • If your passport is lost, report to the police immediately.
  • If your passport will expire soon, renew it before requesting a visa extension.
  • The university cannot transfer visa stamps for students. This must be done by Thai Immigration.
  • Immigration may request additional documents depending on the student’s case.
