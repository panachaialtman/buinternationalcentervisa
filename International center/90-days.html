90 Days Report

The 90-Day Report is a required address notification for foreigners who stay in Thailand for more than 90 consecutive days. It is officially called Notification of Staying in the Kingdom over 90 Days and is submitted by using Form TM.47. This report is not a visa extension and does not give students a new visa period. It is only a notification of the student’s current residential address in Thailand.

Who Needs to Do the 90-Day Report?

Students need to do a 90-Day Report if they:

  • Stay in Thailand continuously for more than 90 days
  • Hold a valid visa or extension of stay
  • Have not left Thailand before the 90-day report due date
  • Are still staying in Thailand under their current permission of stay

    • The day count starts again when a foreigner leaves and re-enters Thailand.

Important Restriction

The 90-Day Report is the student’s personal responsibility.

The university may provide guidance and the TM.47 form, but students must make sure that they submit the report on time. Late reporting may result in a 2,000 baht fine (More of penalty click here)

Students should also understand that:

  • The report should be based on the student’s current residential address.
  • Students who changed address should make sure their accommodation information is updated correctly.
  • Immigration may request additional documents depending on the case.

When to Submit the 90-Day Report

Students should check the due date carefully.

The report can usually be submitted:

  • Within 14 days before the due date, or
  • Within 7 days after the due date

Required Documents

Students should prepare the following documents before submitting the 90-Day Report.

1. Passport

2. Previous 90-Day Report Slip

If the student has done a 90-Day Report before, please prepare the latest 90-day report receipt or slip.

This document is important because it shows:

  • The previous report date
  • The next report due date
  • Immigration record of the last notification

3. TM.47 Form

Students may need to complete Form TM.47, which is the form for notification of staying in Thailand longer than 90 days.

You can get the form at the International Center or at the Immigration office.

4. Accommodation Information

Students should prepare their current address information clearly.

If requested by Immigration, students may also need:

  • TM.30 receipt
  • Rental contract
  • Copy of landlord / owner ID card

5. Copy of Passport Pages

Immigration may require copies of passport pages, such as:

  • Passport information page
  • Current visa page
  • Latest arrival stamp
  • Latest extension stamp
  • Previous 90-day report slip

    • Students should prepare both original documents and copies before visiting Immigration.

Submit in Person at Immigration

Students may go to the Immigration Office responsible for their current residential area.

For example:

Students living in Pathum Thani

Contact Pathum Thani Immigration.

Students living in Bangkok

Contact the Immigration Office responsible for Bangkok.

Students living in another province

Contact the Immigration Office in that province.

Step-by-Step Process

1

Check Your Due Date

Look at your previous 90-day report slip or count from your latest entry date into Thailand.

2

Prepare Required Documents

Prepare your passport, previous 90-day report slip, TM.47 form, and accommodation information.

3

Submit the Report

Submit the report within the allowed period: 14 days before or 7 days after the due date.

4

Keep the Receipt

After the report is completed, students must keep the receipt or approved slip safely.

5

Check the Next Due Date

The next 90-day report date will normally be shown on the receipt.

Important Notes for Students

  • Do not confuse the 90-Day Report with a visa extension
  • Do not wait until the last day
  • Always keep your 90-day report receipt
  • Report based on your current residential address
  • If you moved to another province, check with the correct Immigration Office
  • If you leave Thailand before the due date, you normally do not need to submit the report for that cycle
  • After re-entering Thailand, the 90-day count normally starts again
  • Late submission may result in a fine. Read Penalty
