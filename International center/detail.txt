# NON-ED Visa Extension

* For current students who are still studying
* Used for study purposes only <mark style="color:$danger;">\[Illegal to work with this type of visa]</mark>
* The extension period (6 months / 1 year / other) depends on academic status, payment status, and Immigration approval
* Students must prepare documents before the visa expiry date

***

### **Required documents:**

1. Passport
2. Receipt (Latest tuition fee receipt / registration receipt)
3. Recent photo
4. Accommodation document
   1. If you live outside Pathum Thani: TM.30
   2. If you live inside Pathum Thani: rental contract **and** house owner's ID card copy

<sub>You may bring only your passport first to submit the request and bring the remaining documents on the pickup date</sub>

***

### How to submit request

* Click here to [Non-ED Online submission ](https://internationalcenter.bu.ac.th/Dev/Visa/Visa_Services/login.php)
* Come to the International Center office

***

* Students with academic problems, withdrawal, or (Bachelor) registered less than 9 credits may need additional review

# New Student Visa

International students who plan to study in Thailand should apply for a **Non-Immigrant Visa Category “ED”**, commonly called a **Student Visa**. This visa is for students who have been accepted to study at an educational institution in Thailand. The Non-Immigrant “ED” Visa is issued to applicants who wish to pursue full-time education in public or private educational institutions in Thailand.&#x20;

Students should apply for the visa **before entering Thailand**, normally through the **Royal Thai Embassy / Consulate-General** in their country of residence or through the official Thai E-Visa system, depending on the country where they apply. The Thai E-Visa website is operated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Thailand.

***

### Who Should Read This Page?

This page is for:

* New international students who have been accepted by the university
* Students who are currently outside Thailand and need to apply for a new student visa
* Students who need university documents to support their visa application
* Students who are not sure what documents they need before contacting the International Center

***

| Item                       | Details                                                                                                 |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Visa Type                  | Non-Immigrant Visa                                                                                      |
| Category                   | ED / Education                                                                                          |
| Purpose                    | To study in Thailand                                                                                    |
| Initial Permission of Stay | Usually up to 90 days after entering Thailand, depending on the visa and Immigration officer’s decision |

***

### Important Restriction

The university can issue supporting documents for eligible students, but the university **cannot guarantee visa approval**.

Each Embassy or Consulate may request different supporting documents. Students must check the requirements of the specific Embassy or Consulate where they will apply.

***

### Documents Required to Submit a Request to the International Center

To request university documents for a new student visa, students should prepare the following:

#### 1. Passport Copy

Please provide a clear copy of the passport information page.

The passport should be valid for at least 6 months from the intended travel date. Some Thai Embassy pages list a passport or travel document valid for at least 6 months as a basic requirement for the Non-Immigrant ED Visa.

***

#### 2. Admission Information

Students should provide proof that they have been accepted to study at the university.

This may include:

* BU admission application forms
* Tuition fee receipt / Registration slip

***

#### 3. Embassy / Consulate Information

Students must inform the International Center where they plan to apply or submit BU visa request forms for the visa. Provide the name of the Embassy / Consulate.

Example:

> Royal Thai Embassy, Beijing, China\
> Royal Thai Consulate-General, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam

This information is important because different Embassies may require different university documents.

***

#### 6. Financial Evidence

Some Embassies may request financial evidence, such as a bank statement or sponsorship letter. For example, some Thai Embassy pages list financial evidence or bank statements as part of the application requirements for education visas.

Students should check the exact financial requirement with the Embassy or Thai E-Visa system before submitting the application.

***

### Documents from University

After checking the student’s eligibility and submitted information, the university will prepare supporting documents to student's email such as:

* Letter addressed to the Royal Thai Embassy / Consulate-General
* University registration or authorization documents
* Copy of authorized signer’s document

***

### Common Embassy Requirements

Although requirements may vary, students are commonly asked to prepare:

1. Passport biodata page
2. Recent photograph
3. University supporting letter
4. Proof of current location / Accommodation notification
5. Financial evidence
6. Visa application form

Students should always check the official requirements of the Embassy / Consulate before applying.

# 90 Days Report

The **90-Day Report** is a required address notification for foreigners who stay in Thailand for more than 90 consecutive days. It is officially called **Notification of Staying in the Kingdom over 90 Days** and is submitted by using **Form TM.47**. This report is **not a visa extension** and does not give students a new visa period. It is only a notification of the student’s current residential address in Thailand.&#x20;

***

### Who Needs to Do the 90-Day Report?

Students need to do a 90-Day Report if they:

* Stay in Thailand continuously for more than 90 days
* Hold a valid visa or extension of stay
* Have not left Thailand before the 90-day report due date
* Are still staying in Thailand under their current permission of stay

The day count starts again when a foreigner leaves and re-enters Thailand.

***

### Type of Document

<table><thead><tr><th width="173">Item</th><th>Details</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Document Type</td><td>90-Day Report</td></tr><tr><td>Official Form</td><td>TM.47</td></tr><tr><td>Purpose</td><td>To notify Immigration of the student’s current address in Thailand</td></tr><tr><td>Important Note</td><td>This is not a visa extension</td></tr></tbody></table>

***

### Important Restriction

The 90-Day Report is the student’s personal responsibility.

The university may provide guidance and the TM.47 form, but students must make sure that they submit the report on time. Late reporting may result in a 2,000 baht fine (More of penalty click [here](https://bu-international-center.gitbook.io/bu-international-center-docs/pages/8b99tqf421o0x37USURk#id-2.-late-90-day-report))

Students should also understand that:

* The report should be based on the student’s current residential address.
* Students who changed address should make sure their accommodation information is updated correctly.
* Immigration may request additional documents depending on the case.

***

### When to Submit the 90-Day Report

Students should check the due date carefully.

The report can usually be submitted:

* **Within 14 days before the due date**, or
* **Within 7 days after the due date**

***

### Required Documents

Students should prepare the following documents before submitting the 90-Day Report.

#### 1. Passport

#### 2. Previous 90-Day Report Slip

If the student has done a 90-Day Report before, please prepare the latest 90-day report receipt or slip.

This document is important because it shows:

* The previous report date
* The next report due date
* Immigration record of the last notification

***

#### 3. TM.47 Form

Students may need to complete **Form TM.47**, which is the form for notification of staying in Thailand longer than 90 days.

You can get the form at the International Center or at the Immigration office.

***

#### 4. Accommodation Information

Students should prepare their current address information clearly.

If requested by Immigration, students may also need:

* TM.30 receipt
* Rental contract
* Copy of landlord / owner ID card

***

#### 5. Copy of Passport Pages

Immigration may require copies of passport pages, such as:

* Passport information page
* Current visa page
* Latest arrival stamp
* Latest extension stamp
* Previous 90-day report slip

Students should prepare both original documents and copies before visiting Immigration.

***

### Submit in Person at Immigration

Students may go to the Immigration Office responsible for their current residential area.

For example:

{% endtab %}

{% endtab %}

{% endtab %}

***

{% step %}

{% endstep %}

{% step %}

{% endstep %}

***

### Important Notes for Students

* Do not confuse the 90-Day Report with a visa extension
* Do not wait until the last day
* Always keep your 90-day report receipt
* Report based on your current residential address
* If you moved to another province, check with the correct Immigration Office
* If you leave Thailand before the due date, you normally do not need to submit the report for that cycle
* After re-entering Thailand, the 90-day count normally starts again
* Late submission may result in a fine. Read [Penalty](/bu-international-center-docs/penalty.md#overstay-fine)

# Update / Renew Passport

Students must make sure that their passport is valid and updated before applying for any visa service in Thailand. If your passport is expired, nearly expired, damaged, lost, full, or recently renewed, you must update your passport information with the university and may need to transfer your Thai visa stamp from the old passport to the new passport.

Foreigners in Thailand who need a new passport should contact their national Embassy in Thailand. After receiving the new passport, the Embassy may provide a letter requesting Thai Immigration to transfer the latest visa stamps into the new passport, free of charge.

***

### Who Should Read This Page?

This page is for students who:

* Have renewed their passport
* Plan to renew their passport soon
* Have a passport that will expire soon
* Have no blank pages left in the passport
* Lost their passport
* Have a damaged passport that cannot be read
* Need to apply for a visa extension after receiving a new passport
* Need to update passport information with the university

***

## Transfer Visa Stamp to New Passport

### What Is Visa Stamp Transfer?

Visa stamp transfer means Thai Immigration copies or transfers the important visa and entry information from your old passport to your new passport.

This may include:

* Latest entry stamp (date, port arrival)
* Current visa information
* Latest extension of stay stamp
* Permission of stay information

***

### Where Should Students Transfer the Visa Stamp?

Contact the Immigration Office or International Center to get the Transfer Visa Stamp forms, then bring the forms to the **Pathum Thani Immigration Office**.

***

### Documents Required for Visa Stamp Transfer

Students should prepare:

1. Old passport
2. New passport
3. Copy of old passport page
   1. Information page
   2. Latest arrival stamp
   3. Current visa stamp
4. Copy of new passport information page
5. Transfer stamp form / request form
6. TM.30 or accommodation document, if requested
7. 90-day report slip, if any
8. Student ID, student certificate, or university supporting document, if requested by Immigration

# Penalty

This page explains the common penalties and risks related to:

* [Overstay](#what-is-overstay)
* [Late 90-Day Report](#id-2.-penalty-for-late-90-day-report)
* [Missing or incorrect TM.30 / accommodation record](#id-3.-missing-or-incorrect-tm.30-accommodation-record)
* [Leaving Thailand without re-entry permit requirements](#id-4.-leaving-thailand-without-checking-re-entry-permit)

***

## 1. Overstay

### What is Overstay?

**Overstay** means staying in Thailand after the student’s permitted stay date has already expired.

Students should check the stamp in their passport carefully, especially the date written as:

> **Permitted to stay until**

### Overstay Fine

If a student overstays, the fine is generally:

| Overstay Period                           | Fine                                                                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 day or more                             | 500 Baht per day                                                                                              |
| Maximum fine (Overstay more than 40 days) | 20,000 Baht (maximum) and <mark style="color:$danger;">must leave Thailand and reapply for a new visa</mark> |

### Entry Ban for Overstay

| Overstay Period   | Possible Entry Ban |
| ----------------- | ------------------ |
| More than 90 days | 1 year             |
| More than 1 year  | 3 years            |
| More than 3 years | 5 years            |
| More than 5 years | 10 years           |

#### If the student is arrested for overstay

| Overstay Period  | Possible Entry Ban |
| ---------------- | ------------------ |
| Less than 1 year | 5 years            |
| More than 1 year | 10 years           |

***

### Important Notes for Students

* Do not wait until the visa expiry date to request [visa extension](/bu-international-center-docs/student-visa-extension.md) documents
* Do not assume the university can solve an overstay case
* If your visa is about to expire soon, contact the [International Center](/bu-international-center-docs/intercenter-suggestion-contact.md) immediately
* The university cannot guarantee that Immigration will approve any extension after overstay

***

## 2. Penalty for Late 90-Day Report

If a student does not submit the 90-Day Report on time, the student may receive a fine.

| Case                                    | Possible Fine                                                                                      |
| --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Student reports late by themselves      | Not less than 2,000 Baht                                                                           |
| Student fails to report and is arrested | At least 4,000 Baht, with possible additional fine not exceeding 200 Baht per day until compliance |

***

### Important Notes for Students

* 90-Day Report does not extend the visa.
* Students must still apply for a visa extension separately.
* Students should keep the latest 90-Day Report slip.

***

## 3. Missing or Incorrect TM.30 / Accommodation Record

### What is TM.30?

TM.30 is the accommodation notification for foreigners staying in Thailand. It is usually submitted by the landlord, house owner, dormitory, apartment, or hotel.

House owners, landlords, or hotel managers who host foreign nationals must notify local Immigration within 24 hours of the foreigner’s arrival.

***

### Possible Problem if TM.30 is Missing

If the TM.30 record is missing or incorrect, the student may face problems when requesting Immigration services, such as:

* Visa extension
* 90-Day Report
* Address confirmation
* Immigration document verification

Immigration may strictly request the TM.30 return slip when students go to Immigration for services such as 90-Day Report and visa extension.

***

### Important Notes for Students

* Make sure your landlord, apartment, dormitory, or house owner has completed TM.30.
* If you move to a new address, check whether a new TM.30 is required.
* Your address document should match your current residence.
* If you live outside Pathum Thani, you may need to contact the Immigration Office responsible for your current province.

***

## 4. Leaving Thailand Without Checking Re-entry Permit

### Important Consequence

If a student holds a visa extension in Thailand and wants to travel outside Thailand, the student should check whether a **Re-entry Permit** is required before leaving.

Leaving Thailand without the correct re-entry permission <mark style="color:$danger;">may cause the current permission of stay to become **invalid**</mark>

***

### Students Should Check Before Traveling

Before leaving Thailand, students should check:

* Current visa type
* Current permission of stay date
* Whether the visa is single entry or multiple entry
* Whether a re-entry permit is needed

***

### Important Notes for Students

* Do not leave Thailand without checking your visa status.
* If your visa extension is still valid but you leave without the required re-entry permit, you may lose the remaining permission of stay.
* Contact the International Center or Immigration before traveling if you are not sure

***

## What Students Should Do to Avoid Penalties

### Recommended Actions

* Check your visa expiry date regularly
* Start the [visa extension](/bu-international-center-docs/student-visa-extension.md) process early
* Do not wait until the last day
* [Renew your passport](/bu-international-center-docs/update-renew-passport.md) early if it will expire soon
* Keep your [90-Day](/bu-international-center-docs/90-days-report.md) Report slip
* Confirm that your TM.30 is completed
* Check re-entry permit requirements before leaving Thailand
* Keep copies of all important documents
* Read emails from the International Center carefully
* Contact the International Center if you are unsure.

***

## Quick Penalty Summary

| Issue                                 | Possible Penalty / Consequence                       |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Overstay                              | 500 Baht per day, maximum 20,000 Baht                |
| Serious overstay                      | Possible deportation or entry ban                    |
| Overstay more than 90 days            | Possible 1-year entry ban if reported voluntarily    |
| Arrested for overstay                 | Possible 5-year or 10-year entry ban                 |
| Late 90-Day Report                    | Fine from 2,000 Baht                                 |
| Failure to report 90-Day and arrested | Fine from 4,000 Baht, possible additional daily fine |
| Missing TM.30                         | May affect visa extension or 90-Day Report process   |
| Leaving without re-entry permit       | May lose current permission of stay                  |
| Incorrect or fake documents           | Delay, rejection, or legal consequences              |

***

## Emergency Advice

If your visa has already expired, or you think you may have missed an Immigration deadline:

1. Do not ignore the problem
2. Check your passport stamp immediately
3. Contact the [International Center](/bu-international-center-docs/intercenter-suggestion-contact.md) for guidance
4. Contact the responsible Immigration Office as soon as possible
5. Prepare your passport and all related documents

[International Center](/bu-international-center-docs/intercenter-suggestion-contact.md) can provide guidance and supporting documents when eligible. However, final decisions, fines, and penalties are under the authority of Thai Immigration.
