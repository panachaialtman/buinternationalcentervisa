What ED Plus covers

The Thai Immigration ED Plus framework covers eligible foreign students studying at degree level and eligible graduates. The exact route, documents, and period depend on your academic status and Immigration’s decision.

Choose your current status

Expected to graduate

Check that your remaining credits, academic record, and expected completion status support graduation. The university may use an expected-graduation certificate or other academic evidence for case review.

Study completed, formal approval pending

You may need a pending-approval certificate, transcript, and additional confirmation from the responsible university office. The International Center will determine which document set can be prepared.

Graduation approved

Prepare the official transcript and graduation certificate or other university evidence requested for the post-graduation ED Plus application.

Documents to prepare

  • Original passport Include the passport containing your latest Thai Immigration stamps.
  • Recent photograph Follow the current size and recency specifications provided for your case.
  • Academic evidence matching your status Official transcript and graduation certificate, pending-approval certificate, expected-graduation certificate, or another document specified by BU.
  • Accommodation evidence Outside Pathum Thani: normally TM.30 evidence. Inside Pathum Thani: a valid rental contract and property owner’s identification copy may be requested.
  • Additional evidence requested by Immigration Requirements may change according to the case and current implementation.

Step-by-step process

  1. Check your stay and passport dates

    Begin early enough to renew the passport first when its expiry could shorten the possible extension.

  2. Confirm graduation status

    Use BU systems and the responsible academic office to confirm whether you are expected to graduate, pending approval, or formally graduated.

  3. Submit the ED Plus request

    Provide your passport and available academic evidence through the university system or International Center.

  4. Respond to document requests

    Check your university email regularly. Your status may require a different certificate or an updated transcript.

  5. Collect the BU supporting documents

    Bring the original passport and any remaining required items when collecting the prepared application package.

  6. Apply at Pathum Thani Immigration

    Immigration decides whether ED Plus applies, the period granted, and any additional conditions.

  7. Check every new stamp

    Confirm the permission-to-stay date, status, and any ED Plus re-entry-exemption wording before leaving Immigration.

Passport validity can shorten the result

Two possible outcomes

If you do not renew a passport that expires soon, the extension may only be granted until the passport expiry date. If you renew first and remain otherwise eligible, Immigration may consider the period allowed by the applicable ED Plus policy.

Re-entry under ED Plus

Check for the exemption stamp

The official ED Plus framework includes exemption from obtaining a separate re-entry permit. Rely on this only after Immigration has granted ED Plus and the passport contains the applicable exemption wording. Before travel, verify the current stamp with the International Center when uncertain.

Check the new stamp before leaving

  • Name spelling and passport number
  • Immigration status shown
  • New “permitted to stay until” date
  • ED Plus exemption-of-re-entry wording, where applicable
  • Officer’s stamp and any written conditions

Thai Immigration ED Plus order and supporting-document appendix
Do not wait until after expiry

Graduation or expected graduation does not protect you from overstay. Your current permission-to-stay date remains controlling until Immigration grants a new period.
