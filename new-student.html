NON-ED and ED Plus terminology

The application system, visa confirmation, and Immigration stamp may use category wording such as NON-ED or ED Plus according to the route and current implementation. Select the study purpose and options shown by the official system and the responsible Thai mission. Contact the International Center if the issued document differs from the category BU instructed you to obtain.

Who should use this page?

  • You have been admitted to Bangkok University.
  • You are currently outside Thailand and need the correct student immigration status before travel.
  • You need university supporting documents for a Thai mission or E-Visa application.
  • You have not yet entered Thailand using the visa prepared for your degree study.

Before requesting BU documents

  1. Identify your current location

    The official E-Visa process asks for your passport country, current location, and the Thai mission where you will apply. Choose the mission responsible for that location.

  2. Check that mission’s requirements

    Document lists, financial evidence, residence proof, payment, processing time, and physical-presence requirements can differ by mission.

  3. Confirm your intended arrival plan

    Use realistic travel dates. Do not book non-refundable travel before you understand the mission’s processing conditions.

  4. Request the correct BU package

    Tell the university exactly where you will apply so the supporting letter can be prepared for the correct embassy or consulate.

What to submit to Bangkok University

  • Clear passport biodata-page copy Check that the name, passport number, date of birth, and expiry date are readable.
  • Admission and registration information Provide the BU application or admission documents and tuition or registration evidence requested for your intake.
  • Thai embassy or consulate where you will apply State the complete mission name, for example Royal Thai Embassy, Beijing, or Royal Thai Consulate-General, Ho Chi Minh City.
  • Your current country and planned application period If you are not yet in the country of application, explain when you expect to be there.
  • Intended arrival date and reliable email address Use an email address you check frequently, including the spam or junk folder.

What the university may prepare

After checking eligibility and submitted information, BU may prepare a package containing items such as:

  • Supporting letter addressed to the responsible Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate-General
  • University registration, authorization, or institutional documents
  • Authorized-signatory documents
  • Other university evidence requested for the specific mission
University documents do not guarantee a visa

The Thai mission may request further evidence, conduct additional review, or refuse an application. You remain responsible for completing the official application and meeting the mission’s requirements.

Common personal documents

The official system or mission commonly requests personal evidence in addition to BU documents. The exact list is generated for your application and may include:

  • Passport or travel-document biodata page
  • Recent photograph
  • Evidence of current location or lawful residence
  • Travel and accommodation information
  • Financial evidence or sponsorship evidence
  • Additional documents requested after submission

Thai E-Visa application process

  1. Create and verify your account

    Use the official Thai E-Visa website and an email address you can access throughout processing.

  2. Check eligibility and mission

    Enter passport country, current location, and “Apply at” mission accurately. These selections affect the available route and requirements.

  3. Select the study purpose

    Choose the visa type, purpose of visit, and number of entries displayed for your degree-study application. Follow BU and mission instructions rather than guessing from a similar category.

  4. Complete personal and travel information

    Ensure names, passport details, intended dates, and Thai accommodation information are consistent across every document.

  5. Upload supporting documents and pay

    Upload clear files in the formats and sizes accepted by the system. Payment is generally non-refundable after successful submission.

  6. Monitor requests and download the result

    Check the dashboard and email for additional-document requests. Download and save the e-Visa confirmation when approved.

Before travelling

  • Your English name matches the passport exactly.
  • The passport number is correct.
  • The visa or e-Visa confirmation reflects the study route instructed for you.
  • The visa validity allows entry on your planned date.
  • You have saved digital and printed copies of important documents.

After entering Thailand

Check the Immigration arrival stamp before leaving the airport or border checkpoint.

  • Correct date of entry
  • Correct passport number where shown
  • Expected immigration status or category
  • Correct “permitted to stay until” date
Plan the first extension immediately

The initial permission to stay may be limited. Contact the International Center at least one month before the stamped permission-to-stay date if an extension is required.
