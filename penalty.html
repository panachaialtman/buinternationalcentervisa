Which deadline did you miss?

90-day report due date

Your permission to stay is still valid, but your address report is late. Complete the report in person and prepare for the applicable fine.

Permission-to-stay date

The latest “permitted to stay until” date has passed. This is overstay and requires immediate case-specific action.

BU document deadline

Your stay is still valid, but you requested university documents too late. Contact the International Center immediately and do not assume completion before expiry.

Late 90-day report

Report in person

The Immigration public handbook states that a late reporter must notify Immigration in person and will be fined 2,000 baht. Separately check the passport to confirm that the permission to stay has not expired.

A late TM.47 report does not by itself mean that you have overstayed. It also does not extend the permission-to-stay date.

Overstay consequences

Overstay is an immigration offence

Official Immigration information states that overstay may result in a fine of 500 baht per day, up to 20,000 baht, and may lead to arrest, prosecution, removal, or future entry restrictions depending on the circumstances.

Entry-ban thresholds

The national thresholds below concern entry restrictions. They are different from BU’s operational ability to support a student extension.

Situation Overstay period Possible entry ban stated by Immigration
Voluntary surrender / departure More than 90 days 1 year
More than 1 year 3 years
More than 3 years 5 years
More than 5 years 10 years
Arrested / prosecuted Less than 1 year 5 years
More than 1 year 10 years

BU case-handling guidance

The BU 40-day threshold is not the national entry-ban threshold

For Bangkok University case handling, an overstay exceeding 40 days may mean that the International Center cannot support an in-country student extension and may advise the student to leave Thailand and apply for a new visa. This is university operational guidance; Immigration makes the legal and case decision.

What to do immediately

  1. Check the exact date and count the days

    Use the latest “permitted to stay until” stamp. Do not use the visa sticker validity or a previous extension receipt.

  2. Contact the International Center

    Send your full name, student ID, passport nationality, permission-to-stay date, and clear images of the relevant passport stamps.

  3. Follow the responsible Immigration office’s instruction

    Do not visit multiple offices, leave Thailand, or purchase travel based only on informal advice. The correct action depends on the immigration record and length of overstay.

  4. Prepare funds and documents

    Bring the original passport, student documents, travel evidence when instructed, and sufficient funds for applicable government fines or fees.

  5. Keep evidence of the outcome

    Save all receipts, stamps, departure records, and new visa documents for future applications.

Do not make these assumptions

  • “My 90-day report is valid, so my permission to stay must also be valid.”
  • “My visa sticker has not expired, so I cannot be overstaying.”
  • “Paying the maximum fine means I can remain in Thailand indefinitely.”
  • “Graduation or an ongoing BU request automatically protects me from overstay.”
  • “Leaving Thailand without case review will always solve the problem.”
